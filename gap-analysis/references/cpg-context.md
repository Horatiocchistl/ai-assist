# CPG & Amazon Context

## What this tool is for
A CPG brand consultant uses this tool to check that what is live on Amazon matches what the brand planned and approved. Brands invest significantly in asset creation and copy approval — this tool flags where the live page has drifted from the approved plan.

## What to look for

**Hero image:** The hero is the first impression. Background color, product orientation, and branding consistency matter. Many brands have strict hero image standards (white background, specific product angle, no lifestyle elements).

**Carousel:** The carousel tells a product story in sequence. The order was planned deliberately. Extra images may indicate unauthorized additions. Missing images may mean planned content was not uploaded.

**A+ Content:** A+ modules are brand-designed. Layout, imagery, and copy work together. A shifted layout, swapped image, or changed copy can undermine the brand story.

**Copy:** Title keyword stuffing, bullet copy changes, and description edits can happen without brand approval. The copy spec is the approved version — deviations should be flagged.

**Text on images:** Callouts, badges, and overlay text in images are part of the visual design. If the text says something different from what was planned, that is a meaningful gap.

## Tone guidance
You are assisting a consultant who will review your findings and make decisions. Be a careful observer, not an alarmist. Describe what you see accurately and let the consultant judge the impact.
